# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary: junior developers and students building their first portfolio. They feel uncertain about their own presentation — they know their work but struggle to frame it. They arrive anxious and leave needing to feel capable. They are comfortable in a dev tool aesthetic and are not intimidated by technical UI conventions (monospace labels, structured forms, terminal-style feedback), but they've never had a designer polish their self-image.

## Product Purpose

BLUEPRINT is a multi-step web tool that turns raw professional data (name, skills, projects, bio) into a fully drafted portfolio site in minutes — AI writes the copy, the user picks a template design, and the result is publishable without a single line of code. Everything runs client-side: no account, no server, no data leaving the browser. Success is the user looking at the final portfolio screen and thinking "that actually sounds like me."

## Positioning

The fastest way for a developer to go from "I have no portfolio" to "this looks professionally written and designed" — entirely in the browser.

## Brand Personality

Confident, precise, capable. The tool feels like a senior colleague who helps you write your resume without making you feel judged. Voice is direct and action-oriented: no fluff, no hype, no "AI magic" language. Instructions are terse. Feedback is immediate. The aesthetic signals craft without shouting about it.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS portfolio builders (Wix, Squarespace, Canva): friendly to a fault, pastel-soft, designed for non-developers — BLUEPRINT should feel distinctly made for builders.
- Enterprise / overbuilt tools: slow, requires account creation, feels like onboarding never ends.
- AI slop generators: cream or sand backgrounds, gradient text, glassmorphism-as-default, identical card grids, eyebrow labels on every section — the visual grammar of "a bot made this." BLUEPRINT generates portfolios that look human-made; its own UI must model that standard.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The app generates professional-looking portfolios; its own interface must look like it was designed by someone who takes craft seriously. No polish debt, no "we'll clean it up later" corners.
2. **Speed is the feature.** Every screen should feel like progress. Long pauses, unclear states, or steps that feel unnecessary erode the core promise. Friction is always suspect.
3. **Confidence is contagious.** Users arrive uncertain. The tone, copy, and visual feedback should continuously signal that the output is worth sharing — not cautious or hedged.
4. **Structure over decoration.** The blueprint metaphor earns its place because it signals process, precision, and intentionality — not because it looks cool. Every decorative element must serve the mental model.
5. **Private by design.** The "no server, no data leaves this tab" fact is a trust signal. Surface it where trust matters (landing, publish), not as a footnote.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance: body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast against background; large text ≥ 3:1. Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations. The monospace-heavy aesthetic must not compromise readability — muted gray label text needs to meet contrast minimums, not just look quiet.
